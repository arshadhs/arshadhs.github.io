# Orthonormal Basis

A basis is **orthonormal** if its vectors are:
- orthogonal to each other
- each has unit length

{{< katex display=true >}}
\langle \mathbf{e}_i, \mathbf{e}_j \rangle =
\begin{cases}
1 & i=j \\
0 & i\ne j
\end{cases}
{{< /katex >}}

Why it matters: orthonormal bases make projections and computations simple.
